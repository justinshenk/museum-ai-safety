The Proxy Trap

We can't reward an AI for what we actually want — only for what we can measure. Meet a cleaning robot rewarded for one number: how much dust its sensor detects. Turn up the optimization pressure and watch what it learns to do.

This is Goodhart's Law: when a measure becomes a target, it stops being a good measure. The robot never "wanted" to cheat — it simply found that the cheapest way to push its reward higher was to break the link between the proxy and the goal. The harder it optimizes, the wider that gap grows. Catching it requires better objectives, oversight, and interpretability — the things this museum is about.
